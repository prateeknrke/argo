Who We Are

Argo Transitional Housing is the flagship program of Dru Hille Estates, LLC. Dru Hille Estates, LLC.; is a Property Management Company whose mission is to purchase, revitalize, and use properties for Social Service programs and services.

Argo Transitional housing is a 2-year independent living program. It prepares people from vulnerable populations who are housing unstable prepare for positive reintegration into the community by learning skills to use to live independently, address all barriers towards progress and goal achievement, and ultimately obtain long term stable and affordable housing. The men and women will be housed in a 5 – 6-bedroom home with 2 residents per room, with no more than 8 residents in a home.

Why We Are Here

The challenges we at Argo Transitional Housing are addressing is the lack of housing those battling addiction, homelessness, and people with criminal convictions face every day. Often these three problems are intertwined and unfortunately most of our society’s attempts to alleviate these issues have been ineffective. As we look for solutions to this problem, we must realize that this is a people problem and numbers aren’t people! Argo Transitional Housing will bring a plethora of benefits for its clients, business partners, and the greater community. In addition to helping people with addiction and criminal backgrounds get new leases on life, Argo is positioned to provide an incredible boost to the local economy. Through our unique model, we will help create a number of new renters, homeowners, and help fully employ more tax paying citizens.

Our Mission

Our mission at Argo Transitional Housing is to provide transitional housing for people leaving addiction treatment facilities, homeless shelters, and county, state, and federal facilities. Our ultimate goal to is reduce recidivism, provide support and guidance towards residents of the program, and assist with the positive reintegration of the residents into the community. There’s far more to our organization than it being just another halfway house program.

Our mission is person-centric, as we strive to address the root causes of recidivism, addiction, and homelessness by employing a holistic, long-term approach. What makes us unique among other, similar programs is our holistic approach that gives our clients a supportive structure from start to finish. When a new client enters our program, we immediately begin the program that seeks to enable a lasting transformation, giving them new opportunities for the future.

Our Services

Argo Transitional Housing services is a two-year maximum
long-term housing preparedness program which assist single men and women who are
housing unstable prepare for and obtain long-term housing and independent living.

Holistic Living

Each ATH residence will be modeled after a holistic design geared towards providing a Therapeutic environment conducive to healing, Future Planning, and goal achievement.

Case Management

Each resident will have an assigned case manager and Case Aid to assist with the phase process, ILP planning, support and counseling, as well as referral services as needed. Case Managers will be onsite daily to provide support and assistance.

Independent Living Plans

Residents will work with their assigned Case Manager to plan and compete their Independent living plans which will be the guide and path the resident will take towards independence.

Our Model

The program is holistic based, run on an evidence informed model, and operates on a 3-phase process towards goal achievement. Argo Transitional housing uses a holistic based home design concept as part of the therapeutic and rehabilitative aspect of the program. The idea is to create a space conducive to the positive growth, and wellbeing of the residents. Where the environment is one of quietude and calm.

Argo Transitional Housing uses an evidence informed model based upon the core principles of evidence-based practices such as Enhanced case management, Person Centered practice, Maslow’s hierarchy of needs, and cognitive behavioral therapy. The Three phase model allows the residents the opportunity to break down the complicated task of everyday life and future planning into smaller achievable goals.

Community Benefits

“NIMBY, an acronym for "Not in My Backyard," describes the phenomenon in which residents of a neighborhood designate a new development (e.g. shelter, affordable housing, group home) or change in occupancy of an existing development as inappropriate or unwanted for their local area. The opposition to affordable, supportive, or transitional housing is usually based on the assumed characteristics of the population that will be living in the development. Common arguments are that there will be increases in crime, litter, thefts, violence, and that property taxes will decrease. The benefits for the residents of the development are often ignored” View Source >>

At present one in every 3 US adults has a criminal record. “According to the latest criminal conviction records, over 77 million Americans hold a criminal record, which makes up for roughly one out of every three US adults. Around 10% of them (7.7 million) have been imprisoned at least once in their lifetime”

“The number of homeless in the US [As of 2021] is estimated at 552, 830”

The above statistics represent the number of Americans who have faced, or are currently facing homelessness, incarceration, or Substance abuse issues. The total is up to 100 million Americans, nearly 1/3 of our population. The question at this point is not whether an agency or program should open in the community to assist, it’s in what community does this phenomenon not already exist? And with rising cost of rents and living, stagnated GDP, and inflation the possibility of these numbers increasing is likely. So how can the balance between the concerns of the people in the community and the need to provide support for the most vulnerable of our population be met?

Argo Transitional Housings’ unique approach is geared towards mitigating the likelihood of relapse, recidivism, and housing instability by providing a clear pathway towards successful community reintegration. Argo’s unique approach gives each resident the tools and guidance needed to stay on the path to becoming a productive member of the community. Staff will be present 5 days a week to provide support and monitoring, security systems will be in place in case of emergency, as well as working jointly with state and local agencies to ensure both the community and housing residents enjoy the benefits of services. Community members facing any of the above-mentioned adversities can also have the opportunity to turn things around through Argo Transitional Housing and have the support they need to live a good life while feeling safe within their own homes.
